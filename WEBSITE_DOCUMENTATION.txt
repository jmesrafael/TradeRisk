================================================================================

A. POSITION SIZE CALCULATOR
   ─────────────────────────

   Input Fields:
   ✓ Account Capital - Total trading balance in USD
   ✓ Risk Per Trade - Percentage (%) or USD amount toggle
     - Default: 2% (per professional standards)
     - "Set Default" button saves to local storage
   ✓ Entry Price (Optional) - For precise margin calculations
     - Leave blank for basic calculations
     - Add for exact margin with leverage
   ✓ Stop Loss - Toggle between:
     - Price mode: Exact SL level from chart
     - Percentage mode: Distance from entry (default)
   ✓ Take Profit - Toggle between:
     - Price mode: Exact TP level
     - Percentage mode: Distance from entry (default)
   ✓ Leverage - 1x to 500x (default: 1x)

   Output Calculations:
   ✓ Risk Amount - Maximum $ you can lose on this trade
   ✓ Target Profit - Potential $ gain if TP is hit
   ✓ Total Margin - Capital required (or notional exposure)
     - Without Entry Price: Shows position value you can control
     - With Entry Price: Shows exact margin needed at leverage level
   ✓ Risk/Reward Ratio - Visual tracker (Poor → Average → Good → Great 3R+)
   ✓ Leverage Note - Shows position size and margin at selected leverage

   Calculation Formulas:
   ──────────────────────

   1. Risk Amount Calculation:
      - If Risk Mode = Percentage:
        Risk Amount = (Account Capital × Risk %) ÷ 100

      - If Risk Mode = USD:
        Risk Amount = Entered USD amount

   2. Stop Loss Distance:
      - If SL Mode = Price:
        SL Distance = Entry Price - SL Price

      - If SL Mode = Percentage:
        SL Distance = Entry Price × (SL % ÷ 100)

   3. Position Size (Notional Exposure):
      Notional Position = Risk Amount ÷ (SL % ÷ 100)

      Example: $200 risk ÷ 0.005 (0.5% SL) = $40,000 notional exposure

   4. Required Margin (With Entry Price):
      Position Size = Notional Position ÷ Entry Price
      Required Margin = (Position Size × Entry Price) ÷ Leverage

      Example: $40,000 ÷ $50,000 entry = 0.8 BTC
               At 5x leverage: $40,000 ÷ 5 = $8,000 margin needed

   5. Target Profit:
      - If TP Mode = Price:
        Profit Per Unit = Target Price - Entry Price
        Total Profit = Profit Per Unit × Position Size

      - If TP Mode = Percentage:
        Target Price = Entry × (1 + TP% ÷ 100)
        Total Profit = Calculated same way

   6. Risk/Reward Ratio:
      R:R Ratio = Target Profit ÷ Risk Amount
      Visual bar fills based on ratio quality:
      - 0-1:1 = Poor (red)
      - 1:1-1.5:1 = Average (orange)
      - 1.5:1-2:1 = Good (yellow)
      - 2:1+ = Great (green)

EDUCATIONAL CONTENT
================================================================================

Risk Management Fundamentals Section (6 Cards):

1. "Risk 1–2% per trade"
   - Professional traders rarely risk more than 1–2% per trade
   - Keeps drawdowns manageable
   - Allows survival of losing streaks

2. "Aim for 2R or better"
   - Minimum 2:1 reward-to-risk ratio
   - Only need 34% win rate to break even
   - 50% win rate with 2R = consistent growth

3. "Protect your capital first"
   - Capital preservation is job #1
   - 50% loss requires 100% gain to recover
   - Use calculator every time, no exceptions

4. "Leverage cuts both ways"
   - Amplifies both gains and losses
   - Size position based on SL distance, not account balance
   - Leverage ÷ capital requirement, not position size

5. "Consistency over perfection"
   - One outsized loss can erase weeks of gains
   - Consistent 1–2% risk sizing compounds into edge
   - Impulsive sizing destroys edge

6. "Track every trade"
   - Position sizing is only half the edge
   - Log every trade to discover working setups
   - Patterns emerge quickly from data


FAQ Section (5 Questions):

1. "How does position size affect my risk?"
   - Position size determines gain/loss per price unit
   - Sizing position so SL = risk amount ensures consistent risk
   - Foundation of professional risk management

2. "What's the difference between % and USD risk mode?"
   - % mode: Risk as fraction of account (scales with growth)
   - USD mode: Fixed dollar amount per trade
   - Most traders prefer % for automatic scaling

3. "What is a good risk-to-reward ratio?"
   - Professional target: 1:2 minimum
   - 34% win rate at 1:2 = break even
   - 50% win rate = steady growth
   - 1:3+ gives significant statistical edge

4. "Does leverage change my position size?"
   - Leverage changes margin requirement, NOT risk
   - $100 risk at 10x still = $100 loss
   - But only need 1/10th capital margin
   - Your SL always defines your risk

5. "Why should I use position sizing?"
   - Difference between sustainable trading and blowups
   - Consistent % risk scales with account growth/shrinkage
   - Survive bad stretches and capitalize on winning streaks
